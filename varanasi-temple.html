The spiritual capital of India and one of the oldest continuously inhabited cities in the world

Quick Information

  • Location: Varanasi, Uttar Pradesh
  • Best Time to Visit: October to March
  • Famous Temples: Kashi Vishwanath, Sankat Mochan, Durga Temple
  • Special Attraction: Evening Ganga Aarti at Dashashwamedh Ghat
  • Nearby Attractions: Sarnath (where Buddha gave his first sermon)

About Varanasi Temples

Varanasi, also known as Benares or Kashi, is a city on the banks of the river Ganges in Uttar Pradesh, India. It is one of the oldest continuously inhabited cities in the world and is famous for its many ghats and temples, including the Kashi Vishwanath Temple dedicated to Lord Shiva.

The city is considered one of the holiest places in Hinduism and is believed to be the abode of Lord Shiva. According to Hindu mythology, dying in Varanasi brings salvation (moksha), making it a major pilgrimage destination for Hindus from all over the world.

The Kashi Vishwanath Temple is one of the most famous Hindu temples and is dedicated to Lord Shiva. It stands on the western bank of the holy river Ganga and is one of the twelve Jyotirlingas, the holiest of Shiva temples. The temple has been destroyed and rebuilt several times throughout history, with the current structure built in 1780 by Maharani Ahilyabai Holkar of Indore.

Things to Pack for Varanasi Visit
Essential Items:
  • Modest clothing (shoulders and knees covered)
  • Comfortable walking shoes
  • Hat or scarf for sun protection
  • Sunscreen and sunglasses
  • Hand sanitizer and wet wipes
  • Toilet paper (not always available in public restrooms)
  • Water bottle with filter or purification tablets
  • Basic medications and first aid kit
  • Camera (photography allowed in most areas)
  • ID proof (passport/Aadhar card)
Additional Items (October to March):
  • Light jacket for early mornings and evenings
  • Shawl or scarf for temple visits
Note: Varanasi can be crowded and overwhelming. Keep your belongings secure and be prepared for the sensory experience.
