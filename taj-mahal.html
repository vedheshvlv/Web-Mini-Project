Taj Mahal, Agra

A symbol of eternal love and one of the Seven Wonders of the World

Quick Information

  • Location: Agra, Uttar Pradesh
  • Best Time to Visit: October to March
  • Opening Hours: Sunrise to Sunset (Closed on Fridays)
  • Entry Fee: ₹1,100 for Foreign Tourists, ₹50 for Indian Nationals
  • UNESCO World Heritage Site: Yes (1983)

About Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal is an ivory-white marble mausoleum on the right bank of the river Yamuna in Agra, Uttar Pradesh, India. It was commissioned in 1632 by the Mughal emperor Shah Jahan to house the tomb of his favorite wife, Mumtaz Mahal, who died on 17 June that year, giving birth to their 14th child, Gauhara Begum.

The Taj Mahal was designated as a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1983 for being "the jewel of Muslim art in India and one of the universally admired masterpieces of the world's heritage". It is regarded by many as the best example of Mughal architecture and a symbol of India's rich history.

The Taj Mahal attracts 7–8 million visitors a year and in 2007, it was declared a winner of the New 7 Wonders of the World (2000–2007) initiative.

Things to Pack for Taj Mahal Visit
Essential Items:
  • Comfortable walking shoes
  • Sunscreen (SPF 50+)
  • Sunglasses and hat
  • Light cotton clothes (modest attire recommended)
  • Water bottle
  • Camera with extra batteries
  • Cash for entry tickets and souvenirs
  • ID proof (passport/Aadhar card)
Additional Items (October to March):
  • Light jacket or shawl for early morning visits
  • Moisturizer (winter months can be dry)
Additional Items (April to September):
  • Umbrella or rain jacket (during monsoon)
  • Electrolyte packets
  • Face mist for cooling
Note: Large bags are not allowed inside the Taj Mahal complex. Only small purses and camera bags are permitted.
