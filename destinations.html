Explore India's Iconic Destinations

Taj Mahal, Agra

The Taj Mahal is an ivory-white marble mausoleum on the right bank of the river Yamuna in Agra, Uttar Pradesh, India. It was commissioned in 1632 by the Mughal emperor Shah Jahan to house the tomb of his favorite wife, Mumtaz Mahal.

Best Season to Visit: October to March (Winter season)

Mysore Palace, Karnataka

The Mysore Palace, also known as Amba Vilas Palace, is a historical palace and the royal residence of the Wadiyar dynasty. It is located in Mysore, Karnataka, India. The palace is known for its Indo-Saracenic style of architecture.

Best Season to Visit: September to March

Golden Temple, Amritsar

The Golden Temple, also known as Harmandir Sahib, is a gurdwara located in the city of Amritsar, Punjab, India. It is the preeminent spiritual site of Sikhism. The temple is built around a man-made pool that was completed by the fourth Sikh Guru, Guru Ram Das, in 1577.

Best Season to Visit: October to March

Kashi Vishwanath Temple, Varanasi

The Kashi Vishwanath Temple is one of the most famous Hindu temples dedicated to Lord Shiva. It is located in Vishwanath Gali of Varanasi, Uttar Pradesh, India. The temple stands on the western bank of the holy river Ganga.

Best Season to Visit: October to March

Pondicherry

Pondicherry (now Puducherry) is a Union Territory of India. It was a French colonial settlement in India until 1954. The French influence is preserved in the French Quarter, featuring tree-lined streets, colonial mansions, and chic boutiques.

Best Season to Visit: October to March
